About Me

From the job site to the codebase - my path into tech.

My Story

For seven years I worked with my hands in the field as a licensed plumber, HVAC technician, and backflow tester. Those years taught me problem-solving under pressure, attention to detail, and how to communicate clearly with clients and teammates - skills that transfer directly into software and technical support. I made the deliberate decision to transition into technology. Today I serve as a Financial Technical Support Specialist for a large SaaS company that builds software specifically for the trade industry. Helping other tradespeople succeed with modern tools is deeply rewarding because I understand their world firsthand. At the same time I am completing my second Bachelor of Science degree in Computing Applications at Texas Tech University. Outside of work and school I develop small indie games, explore cybersecurity through platforms like TryHackMe, and continuously expand my full-stack and systems knowledge. My goal is to keep bridging the gap between traditional trades and modern technology - whether that means building better tools for the field, creating engaging games, or hardening systems against threats.

What Drives Me

I believe the best technology is built by people who understand the real problems it solves. My background gives me a unique perspective that pure computer-science paths often miss. I enjoy turning complex requirements into clean, usable solutions and sharing knowledge along the way.
